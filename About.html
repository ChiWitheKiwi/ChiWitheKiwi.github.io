About

Hi, I'm Richard, a San Jose based Filipino American animator who specializes in horror and fantasy.

  • Back

Background

I was born and raised in the Bay Area of California. Growing up in Vallejo, one of the most ethnically diverse cities in the world, I was exposed to different cultures and backgrounds from a young age. This helped to mold my ideologies and empathy for others.

Why Horror?

From the creation of the horror genre, it has always said much about the time it was created. Whether it spoke on the politics, the socio-economic state of the world, or on the feelings of the writer themselves, it was never only about the scares. The terror in conjunction with the messages that horror media conveys is a perfect combo to not only entertain, but also effectively use metaphors and allegories to speak on topics that may be difficult otherwise.

Why Fantasy?

Fantasy is a way to experience things that may not be possible for most who must adhere to the 9-5 life of capitalism. It helps stimulate the imagination and keep the childlike light in us all. It often is able to teach lessons about friendship, trust, understanding, and life in general. It is relatable in the way that we are able to place ourselves in the place of the protagonist, yet magical and mysterious, leaving the audience entranced and engaged.

Why Art?

Art has always been my way of expressing myself since I was little. I was drawn to it ever since I saw paper and pencil, and it allowed me to explore myself and my imagination. As I grew older, and studied not just the technical side of art, but also the meaning of art and its history, I knew it was for me. Art has always been the backbone to a nonmundane life and the best way to spread the passion behind the messages that the artist would like to convey.
